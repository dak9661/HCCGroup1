Lo-Fi Analysis

Olive's Analysis

I tested our index card prototype on two of my residents. They were asked to go through the process of completeing three tasks: getting to assignments for a PSYC 240 class, getting to their Fall 2020 course list and lastly getting to their class list for their ISTE 262 as shown on the index cards. I feel I had to over explain the index cards a bit becasue they weren't familiar with the processes. They were able to complete the tasks after explain with no gudiance. The only issues That I faced when doing this is that they felt the naviagtion was not clear enough/ large enough. The issue was also that for the button to find the classes from previous semesters was called sort instead of smething more descriptive or pertaining to the content. Over all naviagetion is something that that we need work on. It was good to see how a user may interact with our app and know what we should improve before building the hifi prototype.

Kimberly's Analysis

Participant 1 Major: Software engineering
Frame 1 When viewing the landing page instantly said “this is for MyCourses” He knew the purpose of the app
Frame 2 “What if I don’t have an account” Knew what was text input, pressed the login page
Frame 3 Questioned if that was the home page
Frame 4 Confused at first but when he saw the label “priority” and “color” he assumed it was an edit option but didn’t know for what.
Frame 5 Wasn't sure where the frame came from. Selected spring 2022.
Frame 6 “Is this a to-do list?”. He mentioned that would be a cool feature for Mycourses
Frame 7 Knew it was menu-selected content. “Where would I select the menu to get to this page”
Frame 8 “Why do I select the names, what’s the purpose”
Frame 9 Selected the button, questioned if that was the same frame he pressed
Participant 2 Major: Business Management
Frame 1 “It’s a mycourses app”
Frame 2 Logged in no problem. Tapped login button
Frame 3 Took a couple of seconds to figure out what it was, he said because of the lines in the boxes he thought it was crossed out.
Frame 4 Asked if this page is when the user selects the assignments. Wasn’t positive of what the page was.
Frame 5 “Where does the back button go to” he knew that the page was for each semester
Frame 6 Was reading out loud from top to bottom. Said that these are assignments listed that are due
Frame 7 Knew that it was options, he related it to the options are the same from the desktop
Frame 8 He asked if the boxes next to the names were icons or if he can select them. When I asked him if he knew what the page was the said the class list but he wasn't too sure
Frame 9 He viewed the top of the page. After seeing fall 2020 he said that the classes listed are for that semester. He knew the purpose of the page easily.
Analysis: After testing the lo-fi on the 2 participants, Partcipants were confused on where they were at, labeling each page will help users understand the page. Also it seemed that the particpants tried to recall the features used in the mycourses desktop website to complete tasks. Keeping alike layouts from the desktop can help users recognize and improve efficiency and comfort. Labeling buttons will help users as well especially the back button. In addition, having a feature to show where the user is currently at can help users know where they are at. Having a navbar with active state on the icon (active state) they are currently in can help with that

Dylan's Analysis

After testing the lo-fi prototyoe on two participants using scripted questions to guide the test, I found some flaws with the app design. Some of these flaws were due to errors in the lo-fi prototype. The particpants were mostly confused how to interact with the paper model. After some explination the participants procceded. I asked the particpants to complete a task of adding an assignment for a specific class. The users were able to complete the task with few errors. After completion, I asked for feedback. The participants found it difficult to navigate back when making an error but admitted this could be due to the prototype itself and less of the design. After the participants completed the verbal evaluation i aksed them to complete a quick survey. This greatly aided in the new direction for the app. I now know that navigation needs to be improved to allow easier use.
